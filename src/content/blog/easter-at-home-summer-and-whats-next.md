## Easter at Home

This Easter is a little more low-key: just home, family, and a chance to slow down for a minute.

Honestly, that's not a bad thing.

No big production. No trying to force some perfect holiday moment. Just being together, letting the kids enjoy it, and appreciating that home can be enough.

Sometimes the best holidays are the ones that feel simple.

## Work Is Going Well

Work has been in a good rhythm lately.

The projects are moving. The momentum feels real. Not fake-busy, not "we had meetings about meetings" productive — actual progress.

That always changes the mood of everything else. When work is clicking, it gets easier to think clearly, plan ahead, and bring better energy home.

I'm grateful for that right now.

## Getting Active Again

I've been getting more active again, and it's making a difference.

Nothing dramatic. Just doing the obvious stuff more consistently: moving more, working out, not sitting around pretending I'll magically feel better without doing anything about it.

Turns out the boring answer is still the right one.

More movement. More energy. Better headspace.

I'll take it.

## Kids Are Ready for Summer

The kids are *so* ready for summer.

You can feel it already. That end-of-school-year energy is building, and they're counting down the days like it's the biggest event on earth.

And honestly? It's contagious.

Summer with kids always has its own kind of chaos, but it's the good kind. More time together, more chances to get out, more memories that don't have to be complicated to matter.

## New App in Progress

I've got a new app in progress right now.

Still early, still taking shape, but it's one of those projects that feels worth chasing. The kind where each session of work makes the idea clearer instead of more confusing.

That's usually a good sign.

I'm trying to stay disciplined about it: build the real thing, not just the exciting idea of the thing.

## OnCallAgentAdvisorService

One thing I've been working on is **OnCallAgentAdvisorService**.

Yeah, the name is a mouthful. But the work behind it is solid.

It's the kind of project that scratches the right itch for me — practical, technical, useful. The best builds aren't just clever. They actually help someone do something better, faster, or with less friction.

That's the bar.

## The Theme Right Now

If there is a theme right now, it's probably this:

**steady progress beats drama.**

Easter at home. Work going well. More activity. Kids excited for summer. A new app taking shape. Good technical work moving forward.

Nothing here is flashy. That's kind of the point.

Life doesn't have to be chaotic to be meaningful. Sometimes things are just... good. Moving. Building. Growing.

And right now, that feels pretty damn solid.
